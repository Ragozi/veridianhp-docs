# AI and clinical safety

Veridian Motion uses **AI-assisted, non-diagnostic triage** to produce an
**immediate PT-reviewable exercise plan** for licensed physical therapists.
AI output **never** goes directly to patients and **never** constitutes a
diagnosis.

## Design principles

1. **Triage support, not diagnosis.** The product uses *assessment*,
   *observations*, and *PT-reviewed recommendations* — never *diagnosis*.
2. **PT-review gate.** AI generates a draft exercise plan in the provider
   dashboard immediately after intake processing. A licensed PT **reviews and
   approves** before any plan reaches the patient.
3. **Red-flag safety.** Red-flag inputs surface a calm **see medical staff**
   safety action for the patient — never a diagnosis or automated emergency
   dispatch.
4. **Clinical authority.** The PT retains full authority to edit, approve, or
   reject AI output.

<Note>
  AI processing runs **server-side** — not in the patient portal browser.
</Note>

## What AI produces (for PT review)

For each submitted assessment, AI generates a **PT-reviewable exercise plan**
with:

| Field | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| **Name** | Exercise or movement name |
| **Why** | Plain-language rationale (non-diagnostic) |
| **Steps** | How to perform the movement safely |
| **Dosage** | Reps, sets, duration, or frequency guidance |
| **Cautions** | Contraindications or custody-appropriate warnings |

AI may also surface **observations** from the body map, questionnaire, and
photos for PT consideration — all labeled **non-diagnostic**.

## Red flags

When intake responses or AI analysis suggest patterns that warrant in-person
evaluation, the system surfaces:

- A prominent **review first** callout in the **provider dashboard** for the PT
- A calm **see medical staff** safety action for the **patient** — not a
  diagnosis

PTs and custody staff follow facility acute-care protocols. Veridian Motion
does not dispatch emergency services.

## What AI does not do

- Diagnose conditions or replace PT clinical judgment
- Send exercise plans to patients without PT approval
- Initiate external communications
- Store clinical content in application logs

## PT workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review red flags first">
    Address any **see medical staff** / red-flag callouts before approving a
    plan.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review intake and AI plan">
    Compare AI output to body map, questionnaire, and photos. Edit name, why,
    steps, dosage, and cautions as clinical judgment requires.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add PT notes">
    Document the assessment. Mark notes **shared with patient** only when
    appropriate.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Approve and release">
    **Approve** the assessment when ready. The approved plan and shared notes
    become visible in the patient's **My plan / progress** view.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Limitations

Veridian Motion is **not** FDA-cleared software as a medical device. AI may
be wrong, incomplete, or biased. PTs and facilities remain responsible for all
clinical decisions and patient safety.
