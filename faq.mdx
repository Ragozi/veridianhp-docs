# FAQ

<Note>
  **Early-stage documentation.** Answers reflect the product as built today
  and may be updated as the platform evolves.
</Note>

## What is Veridian Health Partners?

The company behind [veridianhp.com](https://veridianhp.com). We build
healthcare technology for jails and correctional facilities. **Veridian
Motion** is our active product (formerly Veridian Health / PostureCare).

## What is Veridian Motion?

A corrections-focused **posture and musculoskeletal triage platform** with
two authenticated surfaces: an **inmate kiosk** (body map, complaint,
four-view photo capture) and a **provider dashboard** (PT assessment review,
assignment, messaging, and plan release).

## How do inmates sign in?

Inmates do **not** self-register. Staff enroll each patient during intake and
hand over an **inmate number** and **initial PIN**. The inmate uses those
credentials on the facility kiosk. Staff can reset the PIN when needed. See
[Inmate kiosk flow](/veridian-motion/inmate-kiosk-flow).

## Do inmates see AI results immediately after submit?

No. After submit, the inmate sees only a **confirmation** and plain-language
safety guidance. AI observations and exercise plans are **not** shown until a
PT marks the case reviewed and explicitly releases content to the inmate. See
[My plan / progress](/veridian-motion/inmate-kiosk-flow#after-submit).

## How do staff sign in?

Staff use **email, password, and TOTP MFA** (authenticator app). Roles are
granted only by an administrator. See
[Roles and access](/veridian-motion/roles-and-access).

## What staff roles exist?

Five roles: **pt**, **admin**, **officer**, **warden**, and **it_admin**.
Each role has scoped access to roster, queue, review, messaging, and settings.
Administrators provision roles and can perform a break-glass MFA reset.

## Is this a live video consult?

No. Veridian Motion is **asynchronous** — kiosk intake followed by provider
dashboard review. Secure internal messaging supports follow-up within the app;
there is no external messaging egress.

## How do providers get access?

PTs and other staff accounts are created by a facility **admin**. Clinical
output requires appropriate role assignment and case assignment from the
unassigned queue. See [Provider dashboard](/veridian-motion/provider-dashboard).

## Who can purchase Veridian Motion?

Jails and correctional facilities that contract with Veridian Health Partners
for the product.

## Is assessment data PHI?

Yes. Complaints, photos, Q&A, notes, and messages are PHI. The application
uses row-level security, signed photo URLs, and audit logging. See
[Data model & privacy](/veridian-motion/data-model-privacy).

## Is Veridian Motion HIPAA-certified?

**No.** The product is designed with **HIPAA-aligned** controls (RLS, audit
log, no PHI in app logs, PT-review gate). A **BAA** is required with the
health provider and any vendor that touches PHI (such as email for PDF export).
CJIS, FedRAMP, GovCloud hosting, MDM kiosk lockdown, and physical controls
are **agency-provided**, not built into the app. See
[Security overview](/security/overview).

## Does the app diagnose inmates?

No. AI output is **non-diagnostic triage support** — observations,
hypotheses, red-flag alerts, and a draft exercise plan. A licensed PT retains
clinical authority; nothing reaches the inmate until reviewed. See
[AI and clinical safety](/veridian-motion/ai-and-clinical-safety).

## Can inmates browse the web from the kiosk?

No. The PWA is hardened for locked kiosks: blocked external navigation, strict
CSP, self-hosted fonts, idle auto-reset. True device lockdown still requires
facility **MDM**. See [Deployment and kiosk](/veridian-motion/deployment-and-kiosk).
