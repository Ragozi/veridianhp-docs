# Integration guide

This is the practical guide. Follow it in order; you'll be in production in
a few days, not a few months.

## Stage 1 — Sandbox (Day 1)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Get sandbox credentials">
    From the dashboard, create a sandbox API key and a sandbox webhook
    endpoint. Both are clearly labeled and cannot move money.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Mint a session from curl">
    Confirm the end-to-end API path works before writing any code. The
    [Quickstart](/quickstart) gives you the exact curl commands.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wire up your webhook handler">
    Implement signature verification and idempotency. See
    [Webhooks](/api/webhooks) for the verified-recipe code.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run a full sandbox payment">
    Mint a session, open the hosted URL, complete the test payment, confirm
    your webhook handler received and processed the event.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Stage 2 — Practice management integration (Days 2-3)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Trigger session creation from your invoice workflow">
    Wherever you finalize an invoice or statement today, add a call to
    create a Veridian session. Store the `sessionId` against the invoice.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Deliver the link">
    Add the `hostedUrl` to the SMS/email you already send patients. If you
    don't send invoices to patients yet, this is the time to add that.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reconcile webhook events">
    On `session.payment.succeeded`, find the invoice by `invoiceId` and
    mark it paid. On `session.payment.failed`, surface the failure in your
    staff workflow so someone can follow up.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Handle reversals">
    On `session.payment.reversed` (uncommon but real), reverse the invoice
    payment and flag the account.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Stage 3 — Branding and patient experience (Day 4)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload your branding">
    From the dashboard, upload your practice logo and confirm the brand
    colors. The Bridge renders these on every patient-facing page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write your patient messaging">
    Customize the SMS/email copy. We recommend including the amount, what
    it's for, and "Pay securely: <link>". Keep it short.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test on a real phone">
    Open a sandbox link on your own phone. Confirm the experience feels
    like your practice, not like a third-party processor.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Stage 4 — Going live (Day 5+)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign the BAA">
    Before you can use live keys, your practice must counter-sign the
    Veridian Business Associate Agreement. The Veridian team sends this
    when you're ready.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Provide your deposit account">
    Veridian needs to know where to send the money. This is a one-time
    setup through the dashboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create live API keys">
    Live keys are prefixed `vbk_live_`. Treat them like database
    credentials — never commit them, never log them.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Switch the host">
    Change your API base URL from sandbox to production. The endpoint
    paths are identical.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run a real payment">
    Send a real invoice to yourself or a friendly first patient. Confirm
    the money lands in your deposit account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Roll out gradually">
    Most practices start with a single provider or a single invoice type,
    confirm everything settles cleanly, then expand.
  </Step>
</Steps>
