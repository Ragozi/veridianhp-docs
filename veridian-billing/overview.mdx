# Veridian Billing

<Note>
  **Coming soon.** Veridian Billing is on the roadmap after Veridian Health
  reaches production with correctional PT consults. This page will expand as
  the product takes shape.
</Note>

Veridian Billing will provide **medical billing and coding infrastructure**
for correctional and community health programs — the API-first billing layer
that Veridian Health Partners is building as its second product.

## What we're planning

Veridian Billing is intended to solve a different problem than Veridian
Health:

| | Veridian Health | Veridian Billing |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Focus** | Clinical access (PT consults) | Revenue cycle (claims, coding) |
| **Primary users** | Facilities, clinicians, inmates | Billing teams, integrators, EHR vendors |
| **Core value** | Get inmates PT care inside the facility | Submit accurate claims without building billing infra |

Planned capabilities include:

- **Coding and claim generation** — translate clinical encounters into
  billable claims with appropriate CPT/ICD codes
- **API-first integration** — embed billing into facility systems, EHRs,
  and third-party workflows
- **Compliance-aware processing** — HIPAA-aligned PHI handling, audit trails,
  and payer-specific rule support
- **Webhook-driven events** — real-time claim status, denials, and remittance
  notifications

## Why it comes second

Veridian Health Partners is sequencing products deliberately:

1. **Veridian Health** — prove clinical delivery in correctional settings
   (PT first)
2. **Veridian Billing** — monetize and operationalize the revenue cycle
   once clinical workflows are established

Clinical data generated through Veridian Health will eventually feed Veridian
Billing, giving facilities an integrated path from consult to claim.

## Shared infrastructure

Veridian Billing will share the same foundation as Veridian Health:

- HIPAA-aligned security and BAA framework
- Unified Veridian Health Partners account and admin experience
- Common audit logging and incident response procedures

See [Security overview](/security/overview) for our current compliance posture.
