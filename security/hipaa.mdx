# HIPAA

Veridian Health Partners processes Protected Health Information (PHI) on
behalf of covered entities — correctional facilities and their healthcare
programs. That makes Veridian a **Business Associate** under HIPAA. We
operate accordingly across all products, starting with Veridian Health.

## What this means in practice

- We sign a **Business Associate Agreement (BAA)** with every facility
  before live PHI flows.
- We implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards aligned
  with the HIPAA Security Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart C).
- We follow the HIPAA Breach Notification Rule (45 CFR Subpart D) for any
  incident that could constitute a breach of unsecured PHI.
- We flow down BAA obligations to any subprocessor that touches PHI.

## What data is treated as PHI

For Veridian Health, PHI includes:

- Inmate/patient identifiers tied to a consult or referral
- Clinical intake information — chief complaint, history, medications,
  functional status
- Visit notes, assessment findings, and treatment recommendations
- Session metadata linking a patient to a clinical encounter
- Audio/video from consult sessions, if recorded per facility policy

What we **do not collect by default**:

- Full incarceration records or criminal history
- Unrelated medical records from outside the Veridian platform
- Custody or disciplinary information unless clinically relevant and
  provided by facility staff in the referral

## Minimum necessary in corrections

The HIPAA minimum-necessary rule requires that PHI use and disclosure be
limited to what's needed for the task. Veridian Health is built around
this principle:

- Referrals require only the clinical context needed for a PT assessment —
  not the inmate's full chart.
- Role-based access ensures custody staff see scheduling logistics, not
  clinical notes, unless explicitly authorized.
- Audit logs record events without copying PHI into log metadata.
- Internal dashboards mask PHI by default; reveal requires per-action
  authorization and audit.

## Patients and their rights

Inmates retain HIPAA rights to access, amend, and receive an accounting of
disclosures of their PHI. Because Veridian is a Business Associate, those
rights are exercised through the facility (the covered entity), not directly
with Veridian. Facilities should route patient requests through their normal
HIPAA workflow; Veridian supplies the data the facility needs to respond.

## Sub-processors

Veridian uses a small number of sub-processors for hosting, telehealth
infrastructure, and communications. Each sub-processor that touches PHI has
a signed BAA with Veridian Health Partners. The current sub-processor list
is available to facilities on request through their account contact.
