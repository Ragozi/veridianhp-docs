# How it works

Veridian Health fits into a jail's existing healthcare workflow. The inmate
never leaves custody. No live appointment is required for the MVP. Medical
staff refer an inmate, the inmate completes a guided self-assessment on a
facility device, and the platform returns PT-driven recommendations.

## The assessment journey

<Steps>
  <Step title="Referral">
    Jail medical staff identify an inmate who would benefit from PT
    assessment — musculoskeletal pain, post-injury recovery, mobility
    limitation, or similar. They create a referral in Veridian Health with
    the minimum clinical context needed.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Inmate preparation">
    Custody escorts the inmate to a facility-controlled device (tablet or
    workstation) in a designated assessment location. Staff confirm the
    session is active and the inmate can begin. Sessions are monitored per
    jail policy.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Four-photo capture">
    The inmate follows on-screen guidance to capture four images:

    - **Front**
    - **Back**
    - **Left**
    - **Right**

    The interface provides positioning prompts so photos are consistent
    enough for analysis — posture, visible asymmetry, and gross movement
    patterns the AI evaluates against PT clinical frameworks.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Tailored questionnaire">
    After photos, the inmate completes a dynamic questionnaire. Questions
    adapt based on earlier answers — chief complaint, pain location and
    severity, injury history, current medications, weight-bearing status,
    and facility-specific constraints (housing unit, equipment access).
  </Step>

  <Step title="AI analysis">
    Veridian Health processes the four images and questionnaire responses
    through an AI-assisted analysis engine built on **PT-driven clinical
    logic** — not generic health advice. The system identifies likely
    musculoskeletal patterns, flags red-flag presentations, and maps
    findings to actionable recommendations.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Recommendations delivered">
    Authorized medical staff receive a structured output:

    - Assessment summary
    - Exercise and mobility guidance adapted for the custody environment
    - Activity modifications and precautions
    - Red-flag alerts requiring in-person evaluation
    - Follow-up and re-assessment suggestions

    Recommendations are available in the dashboard as soon as processing
    completes.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Facility action">
    Medical staff review the output, communicate guidance to the inmate and
    relevant custody or nursing staff, update the health record per jail
    protocol, and schedule a re-assessment or in-person referral if indicated.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Roles in the flow

| Role | Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| **Jail administrator** | Org setup, user provisioning, provider approval |
| **Medical staff** | Referrals, review recommendations, clinical follow-up |
| **Custody staff** | Escort to assessment device, session monitoring |
| **Inmate (patient)** | Completes photos and questionnaire |
| **Approved PT (provider network)** | Enrolled in Veridian; jail-approved for oversight/review as configured |
| **Veridian Health platform** | Photo capture, questionnaire, AI analysis, recommendations, audit trail |

## What stays at the jail

- **Device control** — assessments run on facility hardware, not inmate personal phones.
- **Access decisions** — who gets referred and when they're escorted follow jail policy.
- **Provider approval** — the jail decides which Veridian-enrolled PTs are authorized for their system.
- **Clinical judgment** — AI recommendations support staff; medical personnel retain final care decisions.

## What Veridian handles

- Guided photo capture and questionnaire UX
- AI-assisted analysis with PT-driven recommendation logic
- Secure storage and HIPAA-aligned handling of images and health data
- Provider network enrollment and credentialing infrastructure
- Audit logging of all assessments and access

## Scope boundaries

Veridian Health MVP has clear limits:

- **Not a diagnosis.** Output is PT-driven guidance for facility staff, not a substitute for hands-on clinical examination.
- **Red flags escalate.** Presentations suggesting fracture, cauda equina, infection, or other emergencies trigger alerts — facility staff follow existing acute-care protocols.
- **Hands-on care still required.** Manual therapy, injections, and procedures needing direct contact are out of scope; recommendations include in-person referral when appropriate.
- **No live consult in MVP.** Video or synchronous PT sessions are a future capability.
