# How it works

Veridian Health fits into the facility's existing healthcare workflow. No
inmate leaves custody. No provider drives to the facility. The consult
happens on-site, on a secure connection, with documentation that stays in
the clinical record.

## The consult journey

<Steps>
  <Step title="Referral">
    Facility medical staff identifies an inmate who needs PT — post-injury,
    chronic pain, post-surgical recovery, mobility limitation, or similar.
    They create a referral in Veridian Health with the minimum clinical
    context needed for the consult.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Scheduling">
    The referral enters the consult queue. A licensed PT accepts or is
    assigned based on availability and licensure. The facility receives a
    scheduled session time and prepares the inmate and consult room/device.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Pre-consult intake">
    Before the session, relevant intake information is captured — chief
    complaint, injury history, current medications, mobility status, and
    any facility-specific constraints (e.g., weight-bearing restrictions,
    housing unit limitations).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Live consult">
    The inmate joins the session on a facility-controlled device. The PT
    conducts a remote assessment: history, functional evaluation, exercise
    demonstration, and patient education. Sessions are conducted over an
    encrypted connection; no consumer-grade video tools.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Documentation">
    The PT completes a structured visit note — assessment findings,
    treatment recommendations, home/exercise program guidance adapted for
    the custody environment, and follow-up plan. The note is available to
    authorized facility staff immediately.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Follow-up">
    Depending on the plan, the inmate may receive additional consults,
    on-site exercise guidance from nursing or custody staff (using PT
    instructions), or a referral for in-person evaluation if the case
    requires hands-on intervention beyond telehealth scope.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Roles in the flow

| Role | Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| **Facility administrator** | Org setup, user provisioning, policy configuration |
| **Medical unit staff** | Referrals, scheduling coordination, inmate preparation |
| **Custody staff** | Escort to consult location, monitor session as required |
| **Inmate (patient)** | Participates in consult; receives exercise and care guidance |
| **Licensed PT** | Conducts assessment, documents visit, recommends follow-up |
| **Veridian Health platform** | Scheduling, secure video, documentation, audit trail |

## What stays at the facility

Veridian Health is designed so facilities retain control:

- **Devices and physical environment** — consults run on facility hardware
  in designated spaces, not on personal inmate phones.
- **Access decisions** — who gets referred, when they're escorted, and how
  sessions are monitored follow facility policy.
- **Clinical record ownership** — visit documentation is available to the
  facility's authorized staff; Veridian operates as a Business Associate.

## What Veridian handles

- Secure, HIPAA-aligned telehealth infrastructure
- Provider credentialing and licensure verification
- Session scheduling, reminders, and queue management
- Structured clinical documentation templates
- Audit logging of all platform access and actions

## Scope boundaries

Telehealth PT has limits. Veridian Health is built to recognize them:

- **Hands-on manual therapy** requires in-person care — the platform
  surfaces this in documentation and supports referral for on-site follow-up.
- **Emergency conditions** are out of scope — facility medical staff handle
  acute presentations through existing emergency protocols.
- **Behavioral crises during sessions** follow facility escalation procedures;
  the platform does not replace custody or mental health response teams.
