# Quickstart

This guide walks facility and provider teams through the path from "we want
PT access" to a completed consult on Veridian Health. Timelines vary by
facility size and compliance review; most pilot programs go live within a
few weeks of signing agreements.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Request access">
    Start at [veridianhp.com/request-access](https://veridianhp.com/request-access).
    Tell us your facility type, approximate inmate population, and current
    PT coverage situation. Our team schedules an intro call within a few
    business days.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Complete onboarding">
    **Facilities:** Execute the BAA, provision admin and medical staff
    accounts, and set up consult devices per the setup checklist.

    **Providers:** Complete credentialing, licensure verification, platform
    orientation, and correctional healthcare training.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure your first consult endpoint">
    Designate a secure room with a facility-controlled device. Confirm network
    connectivity and that the device can reach Veridian Health endpoints.
    Run a test session with our team to verify audio, video, and documentation
    flow before scheduling inmate consults.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create your first referral">
    Medical staff submit a referral for an inmate who needs PT assessment.
    Include chief complaint, relevant history, and any facility constraints.
    The referral enters the consult queue for provider assignment.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Run your first consult">
    At the scheduled time, escort the inmate to the consult device. The
    assigned PT joins remotely, conducts the assessment, and completes the
    visit note. Authorized medical staff review the documentation and action
    the care plan.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review and expand">
    After the pilot consult(s), meet with the Veridian team to review
    workflow friction, documentation quality, and staff feedback. Adjust
    configuration as needed, then expand referral criteria and housing units.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Checklist before your first inmate consult

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Agreements and compliance">
    - [ ] BAA signed between facility and Veridian Health Partners
    - [ ] Facility telehealth policy reviewed and aligned
    - [ ] Staff trained on referral and session workflows
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Technical setup">
    - [ ] Consult device provisioned and network-tested
    - [ ] Admin and medical staff accounts active
    - [ ] Test session completed successfully with Veridian support
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Operational readiness">
    - [ ] Custody staff briefed on escort and monitoring procedures
    - [ ] Medical unit knows how to create referrals and access visit notes
    - [ ] At least one licensed PT credentialed and available on the platform
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Sandbox and pilot environments

Veridian Health provides a **pilot environment** for facilities entering
early access. Pilot environments use synthetic or de-identified test data —
no real inmate PHI — so staff can practice the workflow before production
go-live.

Contact your Veridian account contact to request pilot access after
onboarding begins.

## You're done

Once your first consult is complete and documented, your facility has the
full loop working: referral → schedule → session → note → follow-up.
Everything after that is scaling — more referrals, more providers, more
housing units.
