# For facilities

This page covers what jail staff — administrators, medical units, and
operations teams — need to bring Veridian Health online and run the
AI-assisted PT assessment workflow day to day.

## What you get

Veridian Health gives your jail **PT-driven recommendations** without adding
on-site PT headcount or coordinating off-site transport. Medical staff refer
an inmate; the inmate completes four guided photos and a tailored
questionnaire on a facility device; the platform returns structured
recommendations your team can act on immediately.

## Onboarding

<Steps>
  <Step title="Request access">
    Submit a [request access](https://veridianhp.com/request-access) form.
    Our team schedules an intro call to understand your jail type, population,
    current PT coverage, and compliance requirements.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Execute agreements">
    Before any PHI flows, your facility signs a **Business Associate
    Agreement (BAA)** with Veridian Health Partners. Legal teams can review
    and redline as needed.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure your organization">
    A Veridian administrator provisions your jail: organization profile,
    user accounts, role assignments, and assessment device settings.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Approve providers">
    Review and approve licensed PTs from Veridian's provider network for
    your jail system. Only jail-approved, Veridian-enrolled providers appear
    in your configuration. You control which clinicians are authorized.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set up assessment devices">
    Designate one or more secure locations with facility-controlled tablets
    or workstations. Devices must meet your network and security policies;
    our team provides a setup checklist.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Train your staff">
    Medical and custody staff receive orientation on referrals, inmate
    escort, photo capture supervision, and reviewing recommendations.
    Training materials are provided in-platform.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Pilot and expand">
    Most jails start with a pilot cohort — a housing unit or referral type
    — before rolling out facility-wide. We support you through pilot review
    before general availability.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Staff roles

| Role | Access | Typical actions |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Administrator** | Full org settings | User management, provider approval, policy config |
| **Medical staff** | Referrals, recommendations | Create referrals, review PT output, clinical follow-up |
| **Custody staff** | Session logistics | Escort coordination, assessment monitoring |
| **Read-only / audit** | Reports and logs | Compliance review, no clinical actions |

Role assignments follow least-privilege: custody staff see escort and scheduling details, not full clinical recommendations unless explicitly authorized.

## Day-to-day workflow

### Creating a referral

Medical staff identify an inmate who would benefit from PT assessment and
submit a referral through the Veridian Health dashboard:

- Inmate identifier (per your jail's naming convention)
- Chief complaint and relevant history
- Urgency level
- Constraints (housing unit, mobility, security level)

Veridian Health collects the **minimum necessary** PHI. See
[data handling](/security/data-handling) for what we store.

### Running an assessment

1. Medical staff initiate the assessment or queue it for the inmate.
2. Custody escorts the inmate to the designated device.
3. The inmate completes **four guided photos** (front, back, left, right).
4. The inmate completes the **tailored questionnaire**.
5. The platform processes inputs and generates recommendations — typically
   within minutes.

Custody monitors the session per jail policy. The full flow usually takes
10–20 minutes depending on the inmate's mobility and complaint complexity.

### After the assessment

Authorized medical staff review the PT-driven recommendations:

- Assessment summary and exercise guidance
- Activity modifications for the custody environment
- Red-flag alerts requiring in-person evaluation
- Follow-up or re-assessment suggestions

Action items may include communicating exercises to nursing or recreation
staff, scheduling a re-assessment, or referring for hands-on evaluation.

## Facility requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Network** | Stable internet at assessment endpoints; outbound HTTPS required |
| **Devices** | Facility-owned tablets or workstations with camera; no inmate personal devices |
| **Staffing** | Medical staff for referrals and review; custody for escort as required |
| **Provider approval** | Jail approves Veridian-enrolled PTs before they participate in your system |
| **BAA** | Required before production use with real inmate PHI |

## Reporting and audit

Administrators export assessment activity, referral volumes, completion
rates, and audit logs from the dashboard. Logs cover referrals, photo
capture events, recommendation access, and user actions — suitable for
compliance review.
