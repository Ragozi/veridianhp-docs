# For providers

Veridian Health is built around **PT-driven clinical logic** — licensed
physical therapists define the frameworks that power AI-assisted
recommendations. Providers join Veridian's network, then get **approved by
each jail system** before participating in that facility's workflow.

## Who can join the Veridian provider network

To enroll, providers must:

- Hold an active **physical therapy license** in good standing
- Maintain **malpractice coverage** appropriate for the services provided
- Complete Veridian Health **platform orientation** and correctional
  healthcare training
- Pass **background screening** as required by partner jails

Veridian verifies licensure at enrollment and monitors expiration dates.

## Two-step authorization: Veridian + jail approval

Provider access works in two layers:

1. **Veridian network enrollment** — credentialing, training, and platform
   access managed by Veridian Health Partners.
2. **Jail system approval** — each purchasing jail reviews and approves
   which enrolled providers are authorized for their facility.

A provider must be enrolled in Veridian's network **and** approved by a jail
before they can review cases, contribute to clinical oversight, or appear in
that jail's provider configuration. Jails retain control over who practices
in their system.

## Provider role in the MVP

The MVP is **AI-assisted assessment**, not live video consults. Provider
involvement in the first release may include:

- **Clinical framework contribution** — PTs help define the assessment logic,
  red-flag criteria, and recommendation templates the AI applies.
- **Case review** — where jail configuration requires it, approved PTs review
  AI-generated recommendations before they reach medical staff.
- **Quality oversight** — periodic review of assessment outputs to refine
  clinical accuracy.

Live synchronous consults are a future capability, not part of the MVP.

## Enrollment process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Express interest">
    Submit a provider application at
    [veridianhp.com/request-access](https://veridianhp.com/request-access).
    Indicate your licensure state(s) and correctional healthcare experience.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Veridian credentialing">
    Complete licensure verification, malpractice confirmation, background
    screening, and platform orientation.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Join the provider network">
    Once credentialed, you're enrolled in Veridian's provider network and
    visible to jails that use the platform.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Jail approval">
    Each jail you work with reviews and approves your participation in their
    system. Approval is per-facility — enrollment alone does not grant access
    to every jail on the platform.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Clinical standards

Recommendations on Veridian Health follow PT clinical frameworks:

- **Assessment logic** grounded in musculoskeletal evaluation principles
- **Exercise prescriptions** executable within custody constraints — no
  equipment assumptions jails can't meet
- **Red-flag detection** for presentations requiring in-person evaluation
- **Clear scope boundaries** — guidance, not diagnosis; hands-on care
  referred appropriately

## Privacy and professional conduct

- All platform access occurs through Veridian's encrypted environment
- PHI and assessment images are logged and limited to authorized roles
- Report security concerns to
  [security@veridianhp.com](mailto:security@veridianhp.com) immediately
- Professional conduct standards apply as in any clinical setting
