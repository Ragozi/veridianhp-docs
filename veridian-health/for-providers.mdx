# For providers

Veridian Health is built for licensed physical therapists who want to deliver
care in correctional settings through a secure, purpose-built platform — not
ad-hoc video calls, personal email, or undocumented phone consults.

## Who can practice on Veridian Health

To deliver consults on the platform, providers must:

- Hold an active **physical therapy license** in good standing
- Maintain **malpractice coverage** appropriate for telehealth practice
- Complete Veridian Health **platform orientation** and correctional
  healthcare training
- Pass **background screening** as required by partner facilities

Veridian verifies licensure at onboarding and monitors expiration dates.
Consults are matched to providers licensed in the relevant jurisdiction.

## The provider workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review the consult queue">
    Available consults appear in your provider dashboard with referral
    context: chief complaint, relevant history, facility type, and scheduled
    time. Accept consults that match your licensure and availability.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Prepare for the session">
    Review intake information before the session. Correctional consults may
    include constraints you wouldn't see in community practice — housing
    limitations, equipment availability, security protocols. Plan assessments
    and exercise recommendations accordingly.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Conduct the live consult">
    Join the session at the scheduled time. The inmate is on a
    facility-controlled device with custody monitoring per facility policy.
    Conduct your assessment using telehealth-appropriate methods: history,
    observation, functional questioning, exercise demonstration, and patient
    education.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Document the visit">
    Complete the structured visit note immediately after the session.
    Documentation includes assessment findings, treatment recommendations,
    exercise program guidance adapted for the custody environment, follow-up
    plan, and any flags for in-person referral.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Follow up">
    If the care plan requires additional consults, the inmate re-enters the
    queue. For cases that exceed telehealth scope — hands-on manual therapy,
    complex neurological presentation, suspected fracture — document the
    limitation clearly and recommend in-person evaluation through facility
    channels.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Clinical considerations in correctional settings

Practicing PT in a custody environment differs from outpatient clinic work:

- **Environment constraints.** Inmates may not have access to standard
  exercise equipment, private space, or floor-level activities. Recommendations
  should be executable within housing unit or recreation constraints.
- **Continuity of care.** You may see a patient once or twice, not weekly
  for twelve sessions. Prioritize assessment clarity, self-management
  education, and actionable instructions facility staff can reinforce.
- **Custody presence.** Sessions may be monitored by custody staff. Maintain
  professional boundaries and document accordingly.
- **Scope of telehealth.** Remote PT assessment and exercise prescription are
  in scope. Hands-on manual therapy, dry needling, and procedures requiring
  direct contact are not — refer appropriately.

## Documentation standards

Visit notes on Veridian Health follow a structured template designed for
correctional healthcare records:

- **Subjective** — chief complaint, history, pain/function report
- **Objective** — observable findings, functional status, movement assessment
- **Assessment** — clinical impression, telehealth scope determination
- **Plan** — exercises, modifications, follow-up timeline, in-person referral if needed

Notes are available to authorized facility medical staff immediately upon
completion. Incomplete documentation prevents session closure.

## Privacy and professional conduct

- All sessions occur over Veridian's encrypted platform — never personal
  video tools or unencrypted channels.
- PHI access is logged and limited to the minimum necessary for care.
- Providers must report any suspected platform security issue to
  [security@veridianhp.com](mailto:security@veridianhp.com) immediately.
- Professional conduct standards apply as they would in any clinical setting.

## Joining the provider network

Veridian Health is onboarding PTs for early-access facilities. If you're
interested in delivering correctional telehealth consults:

1. Submit a provider interest form at
   [veridianhp.com/request-access](https://veridianhp.com/request-access)
2. Complete credentialing and platform orientation
3. Begin accepting consults from pilot facilities
